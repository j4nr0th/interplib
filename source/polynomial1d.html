1D Polynomial Basis#

The most basic representation of a basis function is the Polynomial1D. This is a class which is defined by a set of coefficients \(A = \left\{ a_0, \dots, a_n \right\}\) as:

\[p(x) = \sum\limits_{i = 0}^n a_i x^i\]

The downside of these is that they become numerically unstable once their degree becomes too large (above 10 or so), as at that point the terms at either side of the power series start to dominate the result. As such, they are not the best choice when polynomials of high degrees are used.

They do however have their advantages:

  • Derivative and Antiderivative are quick to analytically evaluate,

  • Multiplication, division, and remainder are simple,

  • Conversion to and from different representations are easy.

class interplib.Polynomial1D(coefficients: npt.ArrayLike, /)#

Function with increasing integer power basis.

Given a set of coefficients \(\left\{ a_0, \dots, a_n \right\}\), the resulting polynomial will be:

\[p(x) = \sum\limits_{k=0}^n a_k x^k\]
Parameters:

coefficients ((N,) array_like) – Coefficients of the polynomial, starting at the constant term, up to the term with the highest power.

offset_by(self, t: float | np.floating, /) → Polynomial1D#

Compute polynomial offset by specified amount.

The offset polynomial \(p^\prime(t)\) is such that:

\[p^\prime(t) = p(t + t_0),\]

where \(p(t)\) is the original polynomial and \(t_0\) is the offset.

Parameters:

t (float) – Amount by which to offset the polynomial by.

Returns:

Polynomial which has been offset by the specified amount.

Polynomial1D
