Product Basis#

In higher dimensions, the simplest way to create a set of basis functions is to use the set(s) of 1D basis and take their products with each other as the new basis. The advantage of such a choice of basis is the fact that it allows for very natural link with different geometrical forms.

class interplib.BasisProduct2D(b1: Polynomial1D, b2: Polynomial1D)[source]#

A basis function, which is a product of two 1D basis.

Given basis functions \(\phi(x)\) and \(\psi(x)\) with a constant weight \(w\), the product basis function is written as:

\[\Phi(\xi, \eta) = w \cdot \phi(\xi) \cdot \psi(\eta)\]
Parameters:

  • b1 (Polynomial1D) – First basis function.

  • b2 (Polynomial1D) – Second basis function.

classmethod outer_product_basis(basis1: Iterable[Polynomial1D], basis2: Iterable[Polynomial1D] | None = None, /) → tuple[tuple[Self, ...], ...][source]#

Create outer product basis.

Given a set of 1D basis \(\Phi = \left\{\phi_0,\phi_1,\dots,\phi_n\right\}\) and a set of 1D basis \(\Psi = \left\{\psi_0,\psi_1,\dots,\psi_m\right\}\) the set of 2D basis \(p_{i,j} \in P\) is created for:

\[p_{i, j}(\xi, \eta) = \phi_i(\xi) \cdot \psi_j(\eta),\quad i \in [0, n], j \in [0, m]\]
Parameters:

  • basis1 (Iterable of N Basis1D) – Iterable of the 1D basis functions.

  • basis2 (Iterable of M Basis1D, optional) – Iterable of 1D basis functions. If not provided, then the values in basis1 are used instead.

Return type:

(N, M) tuple of BasisProduct2D
